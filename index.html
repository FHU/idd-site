What is Interactive Digital Design

Interactive Digital Design is an interdisciplinary major that combines graphic design and software development. Right-brain + Left-brain come together to make beautiful and functional apps. IDD helps students develop visual design skills and technical software development expertise.

The IDD curriculum begins with 8 core classes, 4 computer science classes and 4 art classes. Then students may choose one of two tracks within the IDD major: developer or designer.

The developer track (B.S.) prepares students who want to specialize in technical aspects of application development. It includes courses in web design, databases, and mobile application development. The designer track (B.A.) emphasizes graphic design and digital photography in its advanced coursework. Both tracks equip students with programming and visual design experience but allow the student to specialize their degree to suit their strengths.

What Kind of Jobs Can I Get

A degree in Interactive Digital Design prepares you for a range of design and development jobs for both the web and mobile app platform. Students with greater technical inclination will be prepared to get jobs as web developers, mobile app developers, and software developers. Graduates with greater interest in design will be prepared to apply those skills to web and app design in addition to positions like user experience designer and user interface designer.
